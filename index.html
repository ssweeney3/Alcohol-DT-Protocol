Alcohol Withdrawal Protocol

CIWA-Ar (Clinical Institute Withdrawal Assessment for Alcohol)

Purpose

The CIWA-Ar scale is a validated tool used to assess the severity of alcohol withdrawal. Scores guide medication dosing and determine the need for higher-level monitoring.

CIWA-Ar Scoring

Score 0–9: Minimal withdrawal — usually no medication required.
Score 10–19: Mild to moderate withdrawal — medication recommended.
Score 20+: Severe withdrawal — high risk for complications. Consider higher-level care.

Assessment Items:

  • Nausea / vomiting
  • Tremor
  • Paroxysmal sweats
  • Anxiety
  • Agitation
  • Tactile disturbances
  • Auditory disturbances
  • Visual disturbances
  • Headache
  • Orientation & clouding of sensorium

Medication Protocol

Example symptom-triggered benzodiazepine protocol:

  • CIWA 8–10: Give lorazepam 1–2 mg PO/IV
  • CIWA 11–15: Give lorazepam 2–4 mg PO/IV
  • CIWA >15: Give lorazepam 4 mg PO/IV

Reassess CIWA every 1–2 hours and repeat dosing as needed based on symptoms.

Escalation Criteria

Consider higher-level monitoring or hospital transfer if any of the following occur:

  • CIWA score persistently >20
  • Uncontrolled agitation
  • Seizures
  • Hallucinations
  • Medical instability (vitals abnormal)
